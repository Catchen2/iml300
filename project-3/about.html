About this website:

Big pharma is a term that refers to the global pharmaceutical industry. Specifically in the U.S., Big Pharma has been leveraging the government's culture of corruption to increase profits while everyday Americans suffer from high drug prices.

At least 1 in 4 of Americans with diabetes have said that they engaged in insulin rationing (using less insulin than is needed in order to make the doses last longer) as a direct result of the skyrocketing price of the drug. A vial of insulin, which is the only life-sustaining option for Type 1 diabetics, retails at around $300.

Epipens, Daraprim, and Insulin are only 3 of over 3400 drugs whose prices have increased significatly faster than inflation.
